Historical Impacts of Severe Convective Weather (SCW)

GIS Project • EAE 557 • December 2024

Project Summary

This project examined historical severe convective weather perils (tornadoes, hail, and damaging wind) and their impacts on communities across the U.S. Using county-level counts, percent changes, and hotspot analysis in ArcGIS Pro, I connected changes in peril occurrence to housing exposure, population, and social vulnerability. The analysis highlights how hazard risk intersects with demographic and housing characteristics, informing resilience and planning decisions.

Research Questions

  • How have historical SCW perils changed between two climatological periods?
  • Where are the statistically significant hotspots of change located?
  • How do these changes overlap with housing unit distribution, population, and social vulnerability?

Methods & Data

Data: County-level counts of tornado, hail, and wind perils; U.S. Census housing and population data; CDC Social Vulnerability Index (SVI).

  • Created county-level peril counts for two climatological periods (P1 and P2)
  • Calculated deltas (count and percent change) between periods
  • Performed hotspot analysis in ArcGIS Pro to identify statistically significant clusters
  • Compared peril changes with housing units, population distribution, and SVI

Results

Reflection & Learning Outcomes

Through this project, I became much more confident using ArcGIS Pro to analyze and solve spatial problems, particularly hotspot analysis. I also learned to critically evaluate remote sensing and gridded datasets, verifying that data is usable before committing to an approach.
