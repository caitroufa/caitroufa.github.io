Case Study: Urban Heat Island Vulnerability in Rockford, IL

Project developed for Remote Sensing Course - December 2023

Research Questions

  • What is the severity of urban heat island (UHI) in Rockford, IL?
  • What neighborhoods are most impacted by UHI?
  • What mitigation strategies can be used to improve UHI and and reduce heat-related impacts to vulnerable communities?

Background

  • UHI - when urban temperatures are greater than the local rural mean temperatures due to:
    • Built environment
    • Reduction in vegetation
    • Anthropogenic waste heat (e.g. vehicle exhaust, AC units, industrial)
    • Weather
    • Geography
  • SUHI - surface UHI uses land surface temps (LSTs) rather than atmospheric temps
  • Exposure to UHI affects human health with over 1300 fatalities per year in the US attributed to extreme heat.
  • SVI - Social Vulnerability Index is a CDC metric used to communicate vulnerability of a community to manmade and natural disasters.
  • HVI - Heat Vulnerability Index is a submetric of SVI that specifically communicates heat related vulnerability.
