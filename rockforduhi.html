Case Study: Urban Heat Island Vulnerability in Rockford, IL

Project developed for Remote Sensing Course - December 2023

Research Questions

  • What is the severity of urban heat island (UHI) in Rockford, IL?
  • What neighborhoods are most impacted by UHI?
  • What mitigation strategies can be used to improve UHI and and reduce heat-related impacts to vulnerable communities?

Background

  • UHI - when urban temperatures are greater than the local rural mean temperatures due to:
    • Built environment
    • Reduction in vegetation
    • Anthropogenic waste heat (e.g. vehicle exhaust, AC units, industrial)
    • Weather
    • Geography
  • SUHI - surface UHI uses land surface temps (LSTs) rather than atmospheric temps
  • Exposure to UHI affects human health with over 1300 fatalities per year in the US attributed to extreme heat.
  • NDVI - Normalized Difference Vegetation Index ranges from -1 to 1 and is calculated from IR and near IR satellite bands and measures the health and density of vegetation with low values representing low density/health and high values representing high density/health.
  • SVI - Social Vulnerability Index is a CDC metric used to communicate vulnerability of a community to manmade and natural disasters.
  • HVI - Heat Vulnerability Index is a submetric of SVI that specifically communicates heat related vulnerability.
    • Redlining was the historical practice of rating a neighborhood for lenders based on race and class and is a contributing factor to both SVI and HVI, with ongoing social, economic, and health impacts.
  • Rockford, IL is a large city in Northern Illinois characterized by historical segregation and redlining practices and large income and demographic differences between neighborhoods.

Methods

Data: Demographics, SVI, and HVI via US Census Bureau and CDC; Redlining boundaries from HOLC 1940's Map; Land use zoning maps from WinGIS; Collection 2, Tier 1, Level 2 dataset from Landsat-8

  • ST Band 10 - Thermal IR
  • QA Pixel - mask out cloud shadows
  • Band 4 - Red
  • Band 5 - NIR

Computations and GIS:

  • Map neighborhoods by zipcode, HVI, and HOLC redlining rating
  • Calculate neighborhood level and citywide SUHI using javascript in Google Earth Engine (GEE) $$ SUHI = meanLST_{urban} - meanLST{rural} $$
  • Calculate NDVI in GEE $$ NDVI = \frac{NIR - Red}{NIR + Red} $$
  • Compare SUHI with NDVI, HVI, and land use to determine vulnerable neighborhoods and potential mitigation strategies.

Results

  • The most vulnerable Rockford neighborhood is 61104
    • Has the highest SUHI 9.4°C
    • On a typical 84°F summer day, temperatures in this neighborhood range between 95 and 100°F
    • High HVI of 24/26
    • Lowest NDVI of 0.267
    • Land use is characterized by embedded industrialization and comercial spaces with few green spaces
