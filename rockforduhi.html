Case Study: Urban Heat Island Vulnerability in Rockford, IL

Project developed for Remote Sensing Course – December 2023

Project Summary

This case study examines the severity and distribution of the urban heat island (UHI) effect in Rockford, Illinois, with a focus on neighborhood-level vulnerability. Using Landsat-8 data, vegetation indices, and social vulnerability metrics, I identified areas where high surface temperatures overlap with social and environmental risk factors. The analysis highlights how historic redlining and land use patterns contribute to present-day disparities and offers strategies to mitigate heat-related health impacts.

Research Questions

  • What is the severity of the urban heat island (UHI) in Rockford, IL?
  • Which neighborhoods are most impacted by UHI?
  • What mitigation strategies could reduce UHI and heat-related risks for vulnerable communities?

Background

  • Urban Heat Island (UHI): when urban areas are warmer than surrounding rural areas due to:
    • Built environment
    • Reduced vegetation
    • Anthropogenic waste heat (e.g., vehicle exhaust, AC units, industry)
    • Weather and geography
  • SUHI: surface UHI, based on land surface temperatures (LSTs) rather than air temperatures.
  • Public health: Extreme heat causes over 1,300 fatalities annually in the U.S.
  • NDVI: Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (–1 to 1), derived from red and near-infrared satellite bands; higher values = denser/healthier vegetation.
  • SVI: Social Vulnerability Index, a CDC metric for community vulnerability to natural/manmade hazards.
  • HVI: Heat Vulnerability Index, a sub-metric of SVI specific to heat vulnerability.
  • Redlining: historic practice of rating neighborhoods for lenders by race/class, contributing to present-day SVI/HVI disparities.
  • Rockford, IL: a large northern Illinois city with a legacy of segregation, redlining, and income disparities across neighborhoods.

Methods

Data: U.S. Census Bureau and CDC (demographics, SVI, HVI); HOLC 1940s redlining maps; WinGIS zoning maps; Landsat-8 Collection 2, Tier 1, Level 2 data.

  • Band 10 – Thermal IR
  • QA Pixel – cloud/shadow masking
  • Band 4 – Red
  • Band 5 – NIR

Analysis:

  • Mapped neighborhoods by ZIP code, HVI, and HOLC redlining rating
  • Calculated SUHI in Google Earth Engine (GEE): $$ SUHI = \overline{LST}_{urban} - \overline{LST}_{rural} $$
  • Calculated NDVI in GEE: $$ NDVI = \frac{NIR - Red}{NIR + Red} $$
  • Compared SUHI with NDVI, HVI, and land use to identify vulnerable neighborhoods and potential mitigation strategies.

Results

Most vulnerable neighborhood: ZIP 61104

  • Highest SUHI: 9.4 °C
  • On an 84 °F day, local temps reach 95–100 °F
  • High HVI (24/26)
  • Lowest NDVI (0.267)
  • Land use: industrial/commercial corridors with limited green space

Mitigation Recommendations

  • Increase vegetation and green space:
    • Vegetation can reduce surface temperatures by 5–25 °C
    • Plant street trees and add landscaped medians in parking lots
    • Create new parks in underused or reclaimed lots
    • Provide free trees/shrubs to residents
  • Improve industrial and commercial practices:
    • Incentivize light-colored/reflective roofs and green roofs (increase reflectance up to 75%)
    • Encourage efficiency upgrades to reduce waste heat
  • Public outreach:
    • Educate residents about heat hazards through libraries, schools, local media, and mailers
    • Create “cool spaces” (extended library hours, splash pads, cooling centers) and provide transport/access support
