Case Study: Effect of a Warming Climate on a Tornado Outbreak Environment Using a Pseudo Pseudo Global Warming Approach

Project developed for Numerical Weather Prediction Course - November 2023

Motivation

What could a tornado outbreak look like in a climate with very little or no anthropogenic warming?
What effect might future warming have on a large tornado outbreak?

Background

In a Pseudo Global Warming (PGW) approach, boundary conditions of a regional climate simulation (RCM) are modified to expected future climate conditions.
For example: $$\Delta T = T(x, y, z)_{\text{future mean}} - T(x, y, z)_{\text{hist mean}}$$ $$T'(x, y, z, t) = T(x, y, z, t) + \Delta T$$
The goal is to compare how environmental conditions for a specific case might differ under different climate conditions.
In this particular case study, I computed a 2°C temperature difference for all locations, thus the extra "pseudo" (what I'm calling PPGW). My proposed purpose of PPGW is for quick inital analysis and hypothesis development prior to PGW or dynamical downscaling.

Case

3.31.2023 Tornado Outbreak
3rd largest tornado outbreak - 142 tornados with 22 in the LOT region - tied for most tornadoes in LOT in a 24 hr period - 11 EF-3's, 1 EF-4

90 mph straight-line winds

27 fatalities

high bulk shear 60-70kts - MLCAPE 1500+ J/kg

NWS - LOT Severe Storm Summary

Methods

Data: ERA5 surface and pressure level
3-31, 0Z to 4-1, 21Z
3 hourly data

Domain: centered on IL
548 pts x 563 pts
3 km grid

Model Simulation: WRF version 4.3.3 with default physics parameters, 1 hour output files
Control run: unmodified ERA5 input data
"Minus" scenario: subtract 2°C from control run metgrid files
"Plus" scenario: add 2°C to control run metgrid files

Computations: WRF CAPE variable output - Plus condition CAPE minus Control CAPE to get a delta, repeat for Minus condition, repeat with Plus condition minus the Minus condition

Results

  • Increases in CAPE with Plus condition and decreases in CAPE with minus condition
  • Minus condition produces fewer and smaller cells
  • Plus condition produces larger cells earlier in the event, then matches the control by 0Z
  • Severe activity for the Minus condition in the southern portion of the domain terminates by 0Z, with an increase in widespread activity in the northern region of the domain
  • Future work: incorporate CIN, 0-6km shear, updraft vertical velocity, and updraft helicity; complete full PGW and compare with PPGW method
