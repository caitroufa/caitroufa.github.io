About Me

Hi! I'm Cait.

I'm a weather and physics nerd on the path to completing a PhD. My journey has been a study in resiliance, coming from a low-income, single-parent home, a first generation college student, working as a custodian, an office assistant, file clerk, retail associate, dance teacher, and conservation worker during college, and later going back to school as a non-traditional student and mom. I understand the value of persistence.

I believe that hard work, tenacity, a collaborative attitude, and kindness have brought me to where I am today. I've experienced weightlessness, starting a microgravity research program in partnership with NASA that is ongoing nearly twenty years later. As an educator, I've created and implemented research-based curriculum for middle school, high school, and college level physics, astronomy, and meteorology courses, connecting with and mentoring hundreds of students in the process. And more recently, I have led a team studying the long-term severe weather perils landscape in the US.

My research interests include large scale climate dynamics, teleconnections, and severe weather climatology. After earning my degree in 2026, my goal is to pursue a research or academic career in atmospheric science or a climate data science position in industry.

I have a BA in Physics, an Illinois Professional Educator's License with endorsements in physics, earth and space sci, and chemistry, and a MA in Physics Education. I have a decade of experience as a science educator, curriculum developer, and content leader/expert, with a background in physics research and engineering (nuclear/chemical, astrophysics, mechanical). In addition to working on my PhD, I am currently completing a graduate certificate in GIS Analysis.

When I'm not engaged in research, you will find me with my family exploring the US in a 1981 VW Westfalia Vanagon, taking photos of the night sky, and listening to live music.
