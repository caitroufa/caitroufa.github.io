Mission Statement

Hi! I'm Cait.

I'm a weather and physics nerd on the path to completing a PhD. My research interests include large scale climate dynamics, teleconnections, and severe weather climatology. I am particularly passionate about mentoring and educating others and in community and climate justice issues. I value hard work, tenacity, kindness, and collaboration and enjoy exploring complex problems. When I am not engaged in research, you will find me exploring the US in a 1981 VW Westfalia Vanagon with my family, taking photos of the sky, hiking or running with my dog, and listening to live music.

Bio

Currently, I am a PhD Student and GTA at Northern Illinois University, where I study atmospheric science and GIS and plan and teach Weather Dynamics 1 and 2. My current research projects within the Weather, Climate, and Society Research Group focus on large scale climate dynamics, and the future severe weather perils landscape in the US.

My journey has been a study in resilience, coming from a low-income, single-parent home, a first generation college student, working multiple jobs as a custodian, retail, office, conservation, and more during college, and later going back to school as a non-traditional student and mom. I understand the value of persistence and ambition, which have led me to many opportunities in science and education. I've experienced weightlessness, starting a microgravity research program in partnership with NASA that is ongoing nearly twenty years later. As an educator for over a decade, I've created and implemented research-based curriculum for middle school, high school, and college level physics, astronomy, and meteorology courses, connecting with and mentoring hundreds of students in the process. And more recently, I have led a team studying the long-term severe weather perils landscape in the US.
