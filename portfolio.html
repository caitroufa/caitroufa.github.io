Research Projects in Atmospheric Science & GIS

Future of Hazardous Convective Weather

Projections of mid- and end-century tornado, hail, and wind environments using high-resolution WRF-BCC climate simulations. Includes hotspot analysis and regional delta mapping. (Coming soon)

Historical Severe Weather & Housing Exposure

Examines 40 years of severe convective weather reports (tornadoes, hail, wind) and quantifies exposure for U.S. housing, mobile homes, and vulnerable populations.

Future Housing Exposure to Severe Weather

Links WRF-BCC model output to U.S. housing datasets to estimate how climate-driven shifts in severe weather may impact housing exposure by mid- and late-century.

Snow Coverage in Northern Illinois

Assesses decadal trends in snow-covered area using Landsat-8 data, NDSI indices, and time-series analysis of winter months.

"Pseudo" Pseudo Global Warming Tornado Case Study

WRF sensitivity simulations of the March 31, 2023 tornado outbreak with ±2 °C temperature perturbations, testing storm environment response to small climate shifts.

Urban Heat Island Vulnerability in Rockford, IL

Integrates Landsat-derived SUHI and NDVI with CDC/US Census vulnerability metrics and historic redlining maps to identify the neighborhoods most at risk.

Education Projects

Atmospheric Dynamics Python Labs

Developed Jupyter-based labs for teaching atmospheric dynamics concepts such as gradients, advection, and deformation using real-world datasets.

STEAM Careers Curriculum

Curriculum design bridging high school physics and earth science with real-world STEM careers, emphasizing role models and pathways in science and engineering.
